## The Story

Terra by Shavindi is a family business with more than 25 years of experience rooted in the earth — literally. Based in Waikkal-Dankotuwa on the western coast of Sri Lanka, the brand brings together two worlds under one roof: the sturdy, time-tested reliability of building materials, and the quiet, handcrafted beauty of terracotta homeware.

The name *Terra* is a nod to the Latin word for earth — the raw material at the heart of everything the brand makes and sells. Every product, whether a roofing tile or a hand-thrown mug, carries the warmth of Sri Lankan soil and the integrity of a business built over generations.

Terra by Shavindi operates on a quotation and enquiry model rather than fixed pricing — reflecting the bespoke, relationship-first nature of the business. Customers enquire, and the team responds personally.

---

## What Terra Does

Terra bridges two product worlds:

### 1. Building Materials (Wholesale)
Structural and construction-grade terracotta and clay products for builders, contractors, and architects. Products include:
- Roofing tiles and ridge tiles
- Handmade bricks
- Hollow blocks
- Wall tiles
- Paving stones
- Clay water filters
- Cement pots

This side of the business is wholesale-oriented, serving construction professionals and large-scale buyers across Sri Lanka.

### 2. Aesthetic Homeware
Handcrafted terracotta and earth-toned pieces for the home, aimed at individuals, interior designers, and lifestyle-conscious buyers. Products include:
- Terracotta mugs and plates
- Serving bowls
- Artisan pots and vases
- Planter pots

This side of the business celebrates slow living, craftsmanship, and the beauty of natural materials.

## Target Audience

Terra by Shavindi speaks to two distinct but overlapping audiences:

### Primary — The Builder / Professional
- Contractors, architects, civil engineers, construction project managers
- Buying in bulk for projects across Sri Lanka
- Values quality, reliability, and a trusted supplier relationship
- Practical decision-maker; needs specs, availability, and pricing quickly
- Likely male, 30–55, established in the construction industry

### Secondary — The Homeware Enthusiast
- Home decorators, interior design enthusiasts, lifestyle buyers
- Drawn to natural materials, artisan aesthetics, and Sri Lankan craft
- Values authenticity, story, and the handmade quality
- May be buying a single piece or furnishing a space with intention
- Likely female, 25–45, urban Sri Lankan or Sri Lankan diaspora
- Influenced by slow living, boho, and organic home aesthetics

### Shared values across both audiences:
- Trust in a business with roots and history
- Preference for local, genuine, Sri Lankan-made
- Appreciation for quality over cheapness
- Personal relationship with the supplier

Terra by Shavindi is:

- **Quiet confidence** — not loud or flashy; lets the quality speak
- **Artisan** — celebrates the handmade, the imperfect, the natural

## Tone of Voice

**Overall feel:** Warm, minimal, poetic without being pretentious.

**Do:**
- Use earthy, tactile language — words like *fired*, *formed*, *rooted*, *shaped*, *from the ground up*, *hand-thrown*, *sun-dried*
- Keep sentences clean and unhurried — Terra doesn't rush
- Let the product breathe — don't over-describe; trust the material
- Speak with quiet authority — 25+ years of experience, not arrogance
- Use *From Earth To You* as an anchor thought when relevant
- Reference Sri Lanka, Waikkal, and the local origin with pride

**Don't:**
- Use corporate, cold, or sales-heavy language
- Overpromise or use superlatives carelessly (*best*, *cheapest*, *number one*)
- Use heavy industry jargon with homeware customers
- Sound mass-produced or generic
- Mention delivery or logistics prominently — the brand offers both in-store pickup and delivery without emphasis on either

**Example copy voice:**
> *From a family workshop in Waikkal-Dankotuwa, we've spent over 25 years learning what the earth gives us — and how to bring it into your home and your build. Terra by Shavindi is that knowledge, shaped by hand.*

| Jet Black | `#2e2d2c` | Primary text, dark UI elements |

### Typography
- **Display / Titles:** Instrument Serif — elegant, editorial, with italic used expressively
- **Body / UI:** Josefin Sans — clean, geometric, modern; used in uppercase with letter-spacing for labels

### Aesthetic Direction
- Minimal boho
- Natural textures, organic shapes
- Warm negative space — nothing feels crowded
- Earth tones throughout — no bright synthetics
- Photography should feel natural-light, tactile, unhurried
- Avoid stock-photo gloss; real materials, real hands, real place

---

## Photography & Content Direction

- **Mood:** Golden hour light, raw textures, hands on clay, unfinished edges
- **Settings:** Workshop, Waikkal landscape, home interiors with natural materials
- **Avoid:** White studio shots, over-edited filters, synthetic backgrounds
- **Product photography:** 3:4 portrait orientation, natural light, neutral earthy backgrounds
- **Hero imagery:** Terracotta pieces as the subject — the product is the star
- **Lifestyle:** Show the product in context — a terracotta mug on a wooden table, tiles on a rooftop, a pot on a windowsill

## What Terra Is Not

- Not a mass manufacturer — this is a family business, not a factory brand
- Not cheap or discount-oriented — quality and craft are the value, not the lowest price
- Not trendy — Terra is timeless; avoid positioning it as "on-trend" or "viral"
- Not impersonal — every enquiry is handled personally; this is not an automated retail experience
